# WTW Launch Market Partner Targets v1

## 1. Purpose
This document is an internal target list for WTW outreach, presentation work, and market planning.

It is meant to help Kwame organize who to approach, how to frame the product, and where the first launch relationships are most likely to land.

## 2. Market Focus
WTW’s first three launch markets are:

- New York City
- New Jersey
- Miami

## 3. Partner Categories
The partner categories WTW should target are:

- Clubs
- Lounges
- Rooftops
- Restaurants
- Dinner-to-night venues
- Promoters / hosts
- Hospitality groups

## 4. Targeting Rule
Real venue names may be used internally for outreach research, preparation, and targeting.

Public site language must stay generic until permission or partnership confirmation exists.

WTW should never imply an official relationship unless that relationship is signed or explicitly approved for public use.

## 5. New York City Target List

### Anchor nightlife targets
Use these for high-status, credibility-building outreach:

- Major downtown clubs
- Rooftop nightlife rooms
- Private member-style lounges
- Established late-night venues

### Restaurant / lounge targets
Use these for dinner-to-night routing and reservation flow:

- Upscale restaurants with nightlife crossover
- Lounge-first dining rooms
- Rooftops with dinner service
- Date-night and group-night destinations

### More realistic first-win targets
Use these for early wins and lower-friction onboarding:

- Mid-size lounges
- Hospitality groups with multiple rooms
- Promoter-led nights that need better routing
- Restaurants already serving birthday and group traffic

### Outreach angle
WTW helps clean up request routing, show demand clearly, and make nightlife easier to manage without noisy DM threads.

The pitch should emphasize:
- curated demand
- concierge routing
- stronger presentation
- cleaner communication
- better feedback loops

## 6. New Jersey Target List

### Jersey City / Hoboken
Target types:
- Rooftops
- Cocktail lounges
- Dinner spots with nightlife crossover
- Private event rooms

Outreach angle:
- position WTW as a premium routing layer for people moving from dinner to the night
- emphasize birthday groups, date nights, and weekend demand

### Atlantic City
Target types:
- Hotels
- Lounges
- Entertainment-driven nightlife venues
- Promoter-friendly rooms

Outreach angle:
- emphasize event visibility and premium access routing
- show how WTW can organize demand before and after dinner or casino-night movement

### Jersey Shore seasonal
Target types:
- Seasonal rooftops
- Beach lounges
- Summer dinner spots
- Weekend nightlife rooms

Outreach angle:
- frame WTW as a seasonal demand router for weekends, birthdays, and group movement
- emphasize cleaner guest handling during high-traffic periods

## 7. Miami Target List

### Anchor nightlife targets
- Premium clubs
- Rooftops
- High-energy lounges
- Late-night destination rooms

### Dinner-to-club targets
- Restaurant concepts that flow into nightlife
- Dinner venues near nightlife corridors
- Group and birthday-friendly spots

### Restaurant/lounge targets
- Lounge-dining hybrids
- Upscale restaurants with strong evening traffic
- Hospitality groups that own multiple rooms

### Outreach angle
WTW should be framed as the nightlife operating layer that captures demand early, routes it cleanly, and helps premium venues understand what the night is doing before the crowd arrives.

## 8. First Business Request
The first ask from a target partner should be:

- founding partner pilot
- request routing contact
- Wave Pass member value
- curated demand routing
- feedback loop

This is the smallest useful request: let WTW route and present demand, then learn from the result.

## 9. What WTW Offers Partners
WTW offers partners:

- organized requests
- guest list, table, and reservation routing
- birthday and group planning demand
- dinner-to-night planning
- feedback insights
- future member traffic
- cleaner communication than promoter texting

The value is operational as much as promotional.

## 10. What WTW Must Not Promise
WTW must not promise:

- guaranteed entry
- guaranteed tables
- official partnership unless signed
- instant confirmation
- fixed pricing or minimums
- confirmed availability without partner approval

The product should stay premium and honest.

## 11. Outreach Priority
Use this ranking:

A. Warm relationships / reachable managers  
B. Mid-size lounges and restaurants  
C. Promoters and birthday hosts  
D. Major clubs and hospitality groups  
E. Investor / strategic targets

This keeps early outreach focused on the fastest path to a real operational win.
